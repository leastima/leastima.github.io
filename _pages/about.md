I am a Master's student in Data Science (MSE) at the **Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics, Johns Hopkins University** (Jan 2026 – Present). \[[Resume](/files/resume4res.pdf)\]



Generally, I'm curious about what phenomena are common across different kinds of models, why these phenomena happen and how I can affect them in various ways. My research broadly falls under **AI for Science**, with two threads: understanding how and why ML models behave the way they do in scientific settings, and building systems that can reason over scientific knowledge.

On the first thread, I have worked on **Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)** — specifically the loss landscape geometry, training dynamics, and transfer learning of physics-informed models — advised by [Prof. Yaoqing Yang](https://web.cs.dartmouth.edu/people/yaoqing-yang) and [Pu Ren](https://paulpuren.github.io/). On the second thread, I am currently working on **scientific claim verification and evaluation**, building multi-agent frameworks that evaluate scientific reports via claim-specific obligation maps and LLM-as-judge pipelines, advised by [Prof. Benjamin Van Durme](https://cs.jhu.edu/~vandurme/) and [William Walden](https://wgantt.github.io/personal-website/) at JHU.

I have also worked on **AI for Health**, designing a self-evolving pipeline for ICU clinical prediction using counterfactual reasoning and confidence-gated memory injection, advised by [Xing Han](https://aaronhan223.github.io/) at JHU.

Previously, I received my B.E. in Data Science and Big Data Technology from **Tongji University** (2019–2023). Then I worked as a Software Development Engineer at **Beijing Dajia Internet Information Technology (Kuaishou)** (2023–2025), where I built performance analysis tools and maintained a JS container for large-scale mobile systems.
